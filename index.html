Vision

Mission: To create a safer environment for people who use needles.

Values: Integrity, Responsibility, & Service

The improper disposal of needles is a problem in big cities and leads to the unsafe use of needles alongside the increase in injuries relating to improper disposal. Our solution is to provide used needle disposal bins in areas with the highest prevalence of needles in places they donâ€™t belong. People who use needles will drop them into the bin, and in exchange receive a clean needle to prevent them from accessing used needles.

The SmartSwap technology will be similar to a coin machine and vending machine. The needle will be dropped into a small slot (small enough that an entire hand cannot fit through) that will hit the bottom of the machine that has sensors on it to recognize that it is a needle. After it is recognized as a needle, the machine will take a clean needle in a separate compartment of the machine and drop it into a tray for the person to take.

About

Established in January 2024, Safer Streets is a nonprofit dedicated to making a significant impact through our needle exchange program in Baltimore, Maryland. Our mission is to reduce the harm associated with injection drug use and to promote public health and safety. We are committed to providing a compassionate and non-judgmental service that respects the dignity of all individuals. At Safer Streets, we offer a range of services designed to support the well-being of our community:

Needle Exchange:

We provide sterile needles and syringes in exchange for used ones to reduce the risk of HIV, Hepatitis C, and other blood-borne infections through a Safer Street patented technology, SafeSwap. SafeSwap is a safe and effective solution for disposing of used needles by placing exchange bins in areas with the highest prevalence of improperly discarded needles.

Safe Disposal:

SafeSwap ensures the safe disposal of used injection equipment, helping to keep our streets and neighborhoods clean and safe.

Education:

Safer Streets educates participants on overdose prevention, safer injection practices, and the risks associated with sharing needles.

Safer Streets believes in a harm reduction approach that acknowledges the challenges of drug dependency while working to minimize its adverse effects. We strive to engage marginalized populations, offering a pathway to healthier lives and community integration. Since our founding in January of 2024, Safer Streets has made significant strides in reducing the spread of infectious diseases and connecting individuals to vital health services. Our program has become a trusted resource in Baltimore, demonstrating that harm reduction is an effective public health strategy.

Products & Services

OUR PRODUCT:

Safer Streets' product called SafeSwap is a needle disposal and exchange machine that swaps used needles for clean needles. This provides safe and proper disposal of used needles while providing users of needles with clean ones.

How it works:

SafeSwap is equipped with sensors and coils that are controlled by electric motors to push out a new needle once a used needle is dropped into the machine. The VisiLOCK technology prevents the needles from being retrieved from the bins once they are disposed of. Where to find SafeSwap: SafeSwap is currently available in local parks, public libraries, public restrooms, and along crowded streets of Baltimore, MD.

OUR SERVICES:

Along with our product SafeSwap, Safer Streets provides educational programming for people to learn more about SafeSwap, needle disposal, and safer drug use. Educational programming will take place at local community centers and public libraries in Baltimore. Each programming event will be led by Safer Streets employees and volunteers who will provide research-backed information on how to improve the problem of needle disposal in Baltimore. To get involved visit SaferStreets.com to sign up as a volunteer or donate to make our programming events as helpful, interactive, and engaging as possible. Our next programming event will take place at the Baltimore Public Library on June 1, 2024. Join us at 4 pm to learn more about how to properly dispose of needles with a side of pizza!

  • 350,000 regular injection drug users in America: All at increased risk of contracting and spreading fatal blood-borne diseases.
  • Costs: It costs an average city about $160,000 to run a Needle Exchange Program (NEP) (about $20 per user per year), whereas one syringe-infected AIDS patient will require upwards of $120,000 per year in public health expenditures.
  • Effectiveness: NEP participants have been found five times more likely to enter drug treatment than those who had never used an exchange.
  • Impact: NEPs throughout the country have reduced HIV transmission rates by one-third to two-fifths.
  • Local Data: 260 heroin-related overdose deaths in Baltimore in 2016.
